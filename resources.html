What Defines Life?

The question of what defines life has intrigued scientists and philosophers for centuries. Life is often characterized by certain biological processes such as metabolism, growth, reproduction, and response to stimuli. However, these criteria are sometimes challenged by entities like viruses, which exhibit some but not all characteristics of life.

NASA provides a comprehensive definition of life that emphasizes the ability to undergo Darwinian evolution as a key aspect. This perspective broadens the traditional biological view and is especially useful in the search for extraterrestrial life.

Encyclopedia Britannica offers an extensive overview of life that includes molecular biology, ecology, and evolutionary theory, providing a multidisciplinary understanding of the concept.

Modern research continues to explore the boundaries of life, including synthetic biology and artificial life, which challenge and expand our understanding of what it means to be alive.
